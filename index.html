Page 1: Wage and Salary Employment Rate by Gender in the World


This scene shows the discrepancy in worldwide workers that are wage and salaried workers as a percentage of total employment.
There are a few key takeaways:

  1. The overall trend is that the percentage of wage and salaried workers has been increasing across both genders.
  2. I had expected that the percentage of women workers would be lower than the percentage of male workers, but this chart shows the opposite to be true worldwide.
Legend: Female line: pink, Male line: blue.

Page 2: Wage and Salary Employment Rate by Gender in the US


This scene shows the discrepancy between wage and salaried workers in the USA. There are a few key takeaways:

  1. As it is worldwide, the overall trend is that the percentage of wage and salaried workers has been increasing across both genders.
  2. The percentage of women workers is still higher than the percentage of male workers, as it is worldwide. This is interesting because there is still a gender pay gap, causing women to be paid less, but more women are actually working.
  3. The percentages (y-axis) of working people across both genders is much higher than the world population.
